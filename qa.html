My Connection to the Winter Olympics

This page is a quick Q&A about how I connect with the Winter Olympics as a fan.

I became a fan from watching the Winter Olympics on TV and seeing how intense the sports are. Events like hockey, skiing, and speed skating are fast and high-skill, and it’s fun to see the big moments and underdog wins.

I love the variety. One minute it’s a precision sport like figure skating, and the next it’s pure speed like luge or downhill skiing. Also, the atmosphere feels different than most sports because it’s country vs. country and it feels like everyone is representing something bigger than just a team.

I would recommend starting with a few easy-to-follow sports: ice hockey, snowboarding, figure skating, and alpine skiing. Watch a short highlight video first, then watch a full event so you understand how scoring works.

Do you have any favorite fan communities or creators?

There are several active online communities where fans discuss Winter Olympic sports, share highlights, and follow qualification news. Here are a few of my favorites:

  • Fan26 (Official Digital Community): The primary official community for the 2026 Games. Members get early access to ticket news, exclusive digital content, and interactive features like sports trivia.
    URL: https://fan26.olympics.com
  • r/olympics (Reddit): The most active general fan community, featuring real-time discussion threads, live updates, and millions of users sharing highlights and commentary.
    URL: https://www.reddit.com/r/olympics/
  • Totallympics: A dedicated forum for Olympic sports enthusiasts, providing detailed qualification news, sport-specific discussions, and coverage of every Winter Olympic discipline.
    URL: https://totallympics.com/forums/
